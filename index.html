WHO ARE WE?

Marine Escort Services Limited (MESL) is a limited liability company incorporated under the Companies Act Chapter 486 of the laws of Kenya. The company is owned by a group of senior retired military officers, and members from other disciplined forces of the Kenyan government.
As such, MESL boasts of the vastness of its pool of professional expertise to ensure unparalleled service in the security industry.

OUR VISION

To provide reliable integrated security solutions with unparalleled service, and quality delivery on land, air, and sea.

OUR MISSION

To develop a global footprint with the capacity to deploy diligent and efficient teams globally to secure and support Land, Air, and Maritime operations

WHY US?

MESL has a countrywide liaison and network outreach, that can enable our clientele to operate with the authority of our assurance on their safety and security.

MODE OF OPERATION

Private-Public Partnerships

The company has programmed to develop Institutional Public-Private Partnerships in the management of Integrated Security Solutions by building synergies with other Government Agencies.
To this end, MESL sought and was granted authority to operate Armed Marine Escort Guards vide letter SEC.PO.L 22/12/5/(59) Dated 20th January 2011(Attached herewith for ease of reference) from The Ministry of Interior & Coordination of National Government. Over the period of time, MESL has diversified to offer other services as stipulated above. It is for this reason that we kindly request the following institutions to assist MESL its mandate in the following areas:


1. Regional Coordinators and County Commissioners-: To facilitate easy integration and cooperation with Government Security Agents especially in the North Eastern Region which is prone to terrorist attacks.
2. The Kenya Firearms Bureau-: Facilatate in issuing Import/Export and Temporary Firearm Licences well before the ship arrives at the port for the sake of verification.
3. The Immigration-: Issue Temporary Permits and visas for the visiting and transit crew onboard, well before the ship's arrival and subsequent docking.
4. The Kenya Police Service:- Safe storage of weapons in the Police Armoury at the Kilindini Port and law enforcement where there is culpability by a visiting Vessel.
5. The KRA and Customs Department:- Verification of weapons on board the vessel and enforcement of due payments to be made to the exchequer.
6. The Kenya Maritime Authority – Obtain information on all incoming Merchant Ships with Armed Guards Onboard through their local Agents.
